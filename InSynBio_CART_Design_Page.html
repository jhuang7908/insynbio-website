AI-Native Design Platform

Our ACTES platform leverages a proprietary library of nearly 200 curated components across 10 categories. We don't just assemble; we match.

  • Antigen Knowledge Base — A continuously updated database of common epitopes, antigen density thresholds, heterogeneity profiles, disease context (solid vs. hematologic vs. autoimmune), and specificity considerations (e.g. on-target off-tumor risk) to guide precise design.
  • Smart Vector Logic — Algorithmic selection between Lentiviral (Standard), Non-Viral/Transposon (Large Payload), and In Vivo LNP (Accessibility) based on clinical needs.
  • Epitope-Hinge Matching — We measure epitope-to-membrane distance using AlphaFold2 to select the precise Hinge length (Short/Medium/Long).
  • Kinetic Engineering — We tune Binder affinity (Kd) and off-rates (koff) via computational mutagenesis. We increase affinity for low-density targets, but tune it down for targets like HER2 to spare normal tissues, while ensuring fast dissociation to prevent exhaustion.
  • Density-Signaling Logic — We analyze antigen density to select the optimal Transmembrane (CD8α vs CD28) and Signaling domains (4-1BB vs CD28).

Therapeutic Architecture Matrix

Our AI engine applies deep biological logic to select the optimal architecture. We don't just assemble parts; we design systems to overcome specific disease barriers.

Disease Context Critical Barriers Advanced Architectural Solution
Solid Tumors (e.g., HCC, Gastric) Physical Exclusion (ECM), Immunosuppression (TGF-β) & On-Target Off-Tumor Matrix-Degrading Synergy: CAR-M to degrade ECM + CAR-T armored with TGFB_DNR. Affinity Tuning (lower Kd on normal-tissue antigens) and Logic-Gated recognition to spare healthy tissue.
Hematologic / Liquid Tumors (e.g., B-ALL, LBCL) Cytokine Storm (CRS) & B-cell Aplasia 4-1BB-Dominant (reduced CRS vs CD28), Safety Switches (tEGFR, iCasp9) for controllable elimination, Tuned Affinity and dose to balance efficacy and toxicity.
AML / Myeloid Malignancies Shared Toxicity (Healthy marrow depletion) & Relapse Transient "Hit-and-Run": mRNA-LNP In Vivo CAR for temporary clearance without permanent aplasia, or Logic-Gated (NOT) circuits.
Autoimmune (SLE, Lupus) Systemic Safety & B-cell Aplasia risks Immune Reset: CD8-Sparing CAR-Treg to induce tolerance, or Short-Lived mRNA CAR to deplete B-cells once, allowing immune reboot.
CNS / Glioblastoma Blood-Brain Barrier & Neurotoxicity (ICANS) Regional Precision: Intracavitary Delivery (ICV) bypasses BBB. Hinge/TM Optimization minimizes tonic signaling to reduce ICANS risk.

Engineering Modules

We assemble the optimal CAR construct using a validated library of functional domains.

  • Advanced Binders — De Novo Mask Design (Computational FTO), Logic-Gated recognition, and Multi-specifics (BiTE/TriTE secretion).
  • Next-Gen Signaling — JAK-STAT (IL-2Rβ) integration (5th Gen), BATF3-programming for stemness, and Synthetic Costimulation (e.g., 4-1BBL/CD40L).
  • In Vivo Delivery — LNP-optimized mRNA (Transient/Safe), Viral-Like Particles (VLP) (Targeted), and AAV (Donor Template) vectors.
  • Allogeneic Engineering — CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing (TRAC/B2M KO) for off-the-shelf universal CAR-T, mitigating GvHD and rejection.
  • Armoring (Payloads) — TGFB_DNR (Anti-suppression), Membrane_IL15 (Persistence), GPX4 (Anti-ferroptosis), Secreted IL-12 (TRUCK).
  • Affinity Tuning — Computational mutagenesis to tune Kd (e.g., lowering affinity for HER2 to spare normal tissue) and k_off (preventing exhaustion).
  • Vector & Capacity — Compact VHH designs to save payload space; PiggyBac / Sleeping Beauty Transposon support for ultra-large constructs (>10kb) surpassing lentiviral limits.

Design Example: GPC3 CAR-T Logic Trace

The ACTES engine doesn't just pick parts; it reasons through the biology. Here is the deduction process for a Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) project.

Input Parameters
Target: GPC3 | Indication: Solid Tumor (HCC) | Delivery: Lentiviral
Step 1: Structural Topology Analysis
• Query: Where is the epitope located on GPC3?
→ Result: Membrane Proximal (< 5nm distance).
💡 AI Decision: Enforce CD8α Short Hinge.
Reasoning: A Long Hinge (e.g., IgG4) would push the CAR too far from the target, preventing effective immunological synapse formation.
Step 2: TME Counter-Strategy
• Query: What are the suppression factors in HCC?
→ Result: High TGF-β + Hypoxia.
💡 AI Decision: Add TGFB_DNR (Dominant Negative Receptor).
Reasoning: Shields CAR-T from TGF-β inhibition, converting the "stop" signal into a survival advantage.
Step 3: Safety & Toxicity
• Query: Is GPC3 expressed on normal tissues?
→ Result: Low expression on normal liver/lung.
💡 AI Decision: Equip iCasp9 Safety Switch.
Reasoning: Mandatory fail-safe to terminate therapy in case of severe on-target/off-tumor toxicity.
Step 4: Kinetics Tuning
• Query: Risk of exhaustion in solid tumor?
→ Result: High. Requires serial killing persistence.
💡 AI Decision: Optimize for Fast Off-Rate (koff).
Reasoning: Prevents chronic signaling and exhaustion, allowing CAR-T cells to detach and engage multiple tumor targets sequentially.

Safety & Efficacy

Safety is engineered into the sequence, not just monitored in the clinic.

  • Safety Switches — iCasp9 (Standard, >95% clearance), tEGFR (ADCC), RQR8 (CD20 mimotope).
  • Logic Gating — SynNotch (AND gate for specificity), iCAR (Inhibitory CAR for normal tissue protection).
  • Toxicity Management — 4-1BB to reduce CRS peak; Local delivery to minimize systemic toxicity.
